Abstract


For my project I will be duplicating the work of Jonathan Cohen, Marc Olano, Dinesh Manocha in their 1998 Siggraph paper entitled Appearance-Preserving Simplification. Their work involves mesh simplification (includng normal maps), based on a new algorithm to determin the "correctness" of the simplification. This new algorithm is based on the actual screen upon which the mesh is to be rendered, and simply uses color, and displacement to determin accuract.

Proposed Schedule


  • Week 1 - Modify my renderer to deal well with the triangle meshes, including texture and normal maps.
  • Weeks 2-3 - Get the mesh simplification working without texture/normal maps
  • Weeks 4-5 - Add texture/normal maps
  • Any remaining time - Get meshes and play with it.
